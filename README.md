Dissociative Recombination (DR) and Dissociative Electron Attachment (DEA) are the processes by which a molecule is dissociated by capturing an electron.
This is known as recombination when the molecule is positively charged (the electron "recombines" to produce a neutral molecule, so to speak) and electron attachment when the molecule is neutral.
DR will produce neutral products, while DEA will typically result in the formation of one negatively charged product.
For example:

-  H${}_2$O${}^+$ $+$ e${}^-$ $\to$ H $+$ H $+$ O
-  HNC${}_3$ $+$ e${}^-$ $\to$ H $+$ C${}_3$N${}^-$

Both DR and DEA can be mediated by the *direct* mechanism.
The scattering electron is directly captured into a dissociative state of the electron-ion system (a dissociative state of the neutral for DR, and a dissociative state of the anion for DEA).
After capture, the system is assumed to proceed along this dissociative state to lower energy, which happens approximately on the timescale of molecular vibration.
This approach is essentially that of Aubin et al. (2026)[^1].

This repository aims to provide a simple program suite to determine direct Dissociative Electron Attachment and Recombination cross sections based on such capture, in any number of symmetries and normal modes of the target.
